Alright enough nonsense what is it you do, woman?

I write css

I speak it fluently. I love it, and how it evolves, like I love my mother tongue.

I've built custom frameworks for award-winning apps, a reusable app framework to whitelabel and rebrand, and for WordPress themes.

I've been at this since 1997. CSS is poetry, it's oxygen, it's love and it's the vessel through which I can make the world a better place. There is nothing I would rather be doing.

I design

I studied psychology to understand how the mind works. I use this to understand how the mind sees the tools I build.

I'm fascinated by how colour changes a mood, instills trust faster than we can grasp what it's doing. I love the feel of shapes, of depth and soul that goes into thoughtful design.

Design is love of art, and form, of function and of reaching out and touching someone you've never met.

I bridge gaps

In all but one company I've worked for in the last 12 years, I have been the bridge that spans design, and programming.

I understand how designers think because I am one. But I code too. So I understand programmer's priorities and what they're up against.

I can take a designer's feeling, carry it carefully through css and html to the programmers to breathe action into it.

I can be the bridge that unites the clans! (I'm so sorry, the Braveheart slipped out there)
